Welcome to the World of Moths

Moths are a group of insects related to butterflies, belonging to the order Lepidoptera. They are fascinating creatures with diverse appearances and behaviors.

Learn more about their important roles in ecosystems, their life cycles, and how you can help conserve these incredible insects.

About Moths

Anatomy of a Moth

Moths have a head, thorax, and abdomen, and they usually have two pairs of wings covered in tiny scales.

Life Cycle

The life cycle of a moth includes egg, larva (caterpillar), pupa (chrysalis), and adult stages.

Behavior and Habits

Moths are primarily nocturnal and are attracted to light sources. They play crucial roles in pollination and as a food source for other animals.

Species

Common Species

Some common species include the Luna Moth, the Atlas Moth, and the Hummingbird Moth.

Rare and Endangered Species

There are several rare and endangered moth species that need our protection. Learn more about them here.

Habitat

Moths can be found in a variety of habitats including forests, meadows, and gardens. Creating a moth-friendly environment in your garden can help support their populations.
